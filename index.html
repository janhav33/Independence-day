The first national flag in India is said to have been hoisted on August 7, 1906, in the Parsee Bagan Square (Green Park) in Kolkata. The flag was composed of three horizontal strips of red, yellow and green.

Shape and colour

The National flag of India is rectangular in shape with tricolour Indian safron, white and green. It has Ashoka Chakra in the middle which is blue in colour. It has 24 spokes which symbolises that we have to work 24 hours a day.

More about Indian flag colour

  • Safforn: Indicating the strength and courage of the country
  • White: Indicating the peace and truth
  • Green: Indicating the fertility, growth and auspiciousness of the land.

Rules To Hoist Indian Flag

  • According to a Supreme Court judgement in 2004, every Indian has the right to fly the national flag freely with respect and dignity.
  • The Tricolour should always be hoisted in a place of power from sunrise to sunset.
  • While hoisting the flag, the saffron band must always be displayed as the topmost band on the right.
  • Using the national flag as decoration is not allowed. So is drawing on the flag or defacing it with an inscription.
  • The flag should not touch the ground or used as an advertisement or wrap. In addition, the flag cannot be torn, damaged or burnt.
